Identity, Values and Strengths

My Values: Growth, transparency, fairness, reciprocity, perseverance.

Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.
I was working at a youth mental health respite facility and having a conversation with my client's mental health nurse. The nurse reviewed the young person's progress and was then handing over information to me after their review. This mental health nurse made extremely judgmental remarks about the young person and called them things like, "stupid", "silly little ****". I could just not say anything about this MH nurse's attitude, unprofessionalism, and misconduct and no one would have known. Given my values of fairness, transparency, and growth in mind, I decided to report this nurse who was senior to me. I also would not want my vulnerable young client to be 'supported' by this nurse given their growth in mind. I am proud of myself with the decision I made when I reflect on this and I would do it again for anyone else.
Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.
I have Indian, Turkish and Iranian ancestry. I was born in southern India and I moved to Auckland when I was 9. I grew up in diverse areas of Auckland and come from a collectivist culture. This exposure has given me a range of unique experiences that shaped my values and identity.
Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.
My compassion allows me to connect with people and this strength makes it easy to collaborate and communicate with people in the course. Having growth as a value drives my curiosity and learning throughout this course; and I also like to support the growth of other's in this journey (especially in bootcamp). My determination is a strength that will help dedicate time consistently to my study.
Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.
I had a different perspective to that of a previous co-corker regarding a team strategy for a rangatahi displaying psychosis symptoms. We both came from a place of genuine concern for our rangatahi and the differing perspectives on team strategy caused tension during a team meeting. We tried to resolve this during the team meeting and when we ran out of time, I asked to have a one on one conversation with my co-worker in order to resolve the conflict. My colleague agreed and so we talked about it privately and came to a shared understanding. We then went back to the team and proposed a new plan that incorporated aspects of both of our ideas in the team strategy and the team was also happy with this proposal. I think that this was a very effective strategy and we actually became friends outside of work afterwards. If our one on one chat had not worked, I would have escalated it to our supervisor and asked for their involvement in facilitating an agreement with my co-worker. This would provide both of us with guidance and support during the process.
