EQ and IQ

What is Emotional Intelligence?
Your ability to problem solve using social awareness, emotional awareness, and emotional regulation. It is a set of vital thinking skills that were not recognised by traditional understandings of IQ.
How is it different to IQ?
IQ stands for Intelligence Quotient while EQ stands for emotional Quotient. IQ measurements focus on capturing a person's intellectual abilities such as using logic, memory, and processing speed abilities. Standardised IQ tests have been developed in order to score a person's IQ. IQ tests cannot measure other critical aspects of human intelligence such as social intelligence, empathy or creativity. This is where EQ comes in!
Why is it important?
The importance of EQ in capturing a more holistic understanding of human intelligence has been raised by researchers strongly in the last 20 years. Emerging research on EQ has lead to the recent development of standardised EQ tests that aim to measure abilities such as self-awareness, social skills and motivation. Traditionally IQ scores have been linked to job performances and academic standing. However, there is ongoing debate as to how accurate this is. Researchers have also pointed out factors such as cultural relevance and biases in historical IQ testing. EQ scores are liked with job satisfaction, less stress, and higher success rates of entering employment. Both IQ and EQ are important in promoting wellness and employers are increasingly measuring both forms of intelligence when recruiting. Studies regarding neuroplasticity have been critical in establishing the importance of EQ. Similar to how people can work on improving their IQ, they can also work on improving their EQ with targeted practice, repetition and commitment.
