Problem Solving

1. Tell your non-tech friend a story about a time you were blocked on a simple problem. What was the problem?
I had to write code for this problem and I just didn't understand what I was doing wrong. I understood logically what I needed to do but I couldn't figure out the syntax and structure of how to actually write the solution.
What problem-solving techniques did you use?
I tried:
  1. Read and understand the problem
  2. Look at the hints, tests, and info in handbook for guidance.
  3. Read recommended links for more clarification.
  4. Go back and read the problem again with all this new info and see if I can solve the problem at this stage.
  5. If I still don't quite understand, then I google for more specific resources and examples.
  6. Then I'd pseudocode to help break things down and brainstorm potential components of a solution.
  7. Practice rubber ducking.
  8. Look on discord to see if others are asking for help about the same problems.
How did you feel throughout the process?
I felt very frustrated throughout the process because I understand the logic and wider concepts but I just don't know how to transform that into code that a computer will accept.
What did you learn?
I learnt that frustrated is going to be an ongoing part of my experience with learning how to code. I learnt console logging to see what my code is looking like and for trouble-shooting. I also learnt that I am a visual learner and attaching abstract concepts to images or stories really helps me learn.
2. Tell your non-tech friend a story about a time you elegantly solved a problem. What was the problem?
After solving the problem I talked about earlier, I had to solve another problem that used this thing called the .filter method. I solved it much more easily after having a better grasp of syntax.
What problem-solving techniques did you use?
I looked at the hints, tests, and info in handbook for guidance. Then I read recommended links and googled for more clarification. I used the rubber duck method as well.
How did you feel throughout the process?
I felt unsure at first. As I problem-solved using the techniques above, I felt like things started to make a bit more sense.
What did you learn?
That I am learning lots of things even though it may not feel like I am learning anything sometimes.
3. Reflect on how confident you feel using each of these problem-solving techniques/processes:
  1. Pseudocode: I am more comfortable using pseudocode and it really helped me break the problem and my approach down. I'm about a 8/10 confident (10 being super confident).
  2. Trying something: I use this when I have exhausted most other avenues and think it feels like a stab in the dark to just try something. I'm about a 6/10 confident but its not my preference as a technique.
  3. Rubber ducky method: This was a really helpful tool surprisingly, and it pointed out to me what my learning gaps are as I talked out loud. I'm about an 8/10 confident with this technique.
  4. Reading error messages: The Replit exercises taught me to see the value of reading error messages and I feel more comfortable reading them now. I'm a 7/10 confident with this one and I want to make a habit of using this technique more often.
  5. Console.logging: I finally understood the purpose of console logging this week. I spent a chunk of my time this week gaining a clearer understanding about what console logging actually does and how it differs to calling or returning. I'm a 6.5/10 confident using this technique.
  6. Googling: This has been my go to technique from previous studies as well, so I'm pretty confident (9/10) with this technique.
  7. Asking your peers for help: I am about a 4/10 confident with asking my peers for help. I don't like to take up people's time and energy and I don't want to sound stupid and I know this is something on my end I need to keep working on.
  8. Asking coaches for help: I am about a 4/10 confident with asking coaches for help for the same reason as above. I am going to work on this by asking my facilitator for a catch up and for help with technical stuff.
  9. Improving your process with reflection: I have used reflection to improve other aspects of my life so this technique is already well integrated in my life. 9/10 for confidence levels for this one.
