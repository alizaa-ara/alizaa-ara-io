Learning Plan Revised

What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?
I would still like to work towards becoming a full stack dev as a long term goal. I am hoping to use the skills I learn in this course to:
  1. Firstly, improve the quality of my life with the financial stability and independence I will gain from entering the industry as a junior/grad dev.
  2. Empower others coming from low socio economic backgrounds and marginalised communities to enter, and become proactive in the industry to better the representation in development of technology
  3. Work on charitable projects outside of employment (such as an app for animal adoption services), in order to help uplift local communities.
  4. Keep learning because there is so much out there!
What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?
After re-evaluation from sprint 2, I think that my strengths around being agreeable, resourceful, empathetic, and dedicated are still valuable. Since Sprint 2, I have gotten to see other strengths I was not as aware of. I've been sick, my dog was injured, and a bunch of other unexpected personal challenges came up during foundations. I persevered through these and managed to complete all my work within the guided deadlines and I am really proud of myself for this.

After re-evaluation from sprint 2, I think my limitations also remain the same with being easily distractable, over-thinking, and not being super confident with my technical abilities. Core work in Foundations helped me discover the extent of the hindrance my negative self talk can have on my productivity and emotions. Now that I am more aware of this, I am going to be mindful of this tendency and work on self-compassion throughout bootcamp and in everyday life.

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?
My biggest non-technical challenge will most likely be overcoming my inner critic and self-judgements throughout Bootcamp. Another challenge for me will be making it on time for early morning starts as my concept of time is all over the place due to ADHD. I have asked my facilitator for linking me with a go-to person for Bootcamp so that I can get support with working through my negative self talk if needed. I have also talked with my Facilitator about getting out for fresh air to do some emotional regulation exercises at the Domain. I am looking forward to engaging in the yoga sessions as another method of self-compassion. I am going to work on waking up early in my revision week so that my body/sleep patterns are better adjusted for Bootcamp. I am ging to do this with writing up a timetable for revision week and setting alarms.
What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?
I would like to see myself develop more confidence in my abilities overall by the end of Bootcamp. This lack of confidence can make me very passive in teamwork as I don't think I have much to contribute. To work on this, I am going to do my best with voicing my ideas even if I am not sure about them.
What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?
I expect to have people I can go to for clarification on coding-related concepts and at times, this may mean that I need a one on one conversation that helps me gain a better understanding. Being able to have discussions on concepts is important for my learning, and it may require a bit of patience from the Bootcamp team from time to time. If I am having a hard mental health day, I hope that the classroom environment is a space where I can safely declare that instead of having to pretend/put a poker face on that takes up a lot of energy and results in less productivity. I expect to be able to give constructive feedback to the team without anyone getting defensive, angry, etc. I also expect to receive feedback constructively so that I can grow as a dev and as a person.
What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp?
For starters, I expect myself to be there on time everyday! I expect myself to engage with people and the material in a respectful manner and to work on taking small break for emotional regulation if I start feeling overwhelmed or anxious. I will ask the team for support, and this will be much easier for me to do when I am there in person. I will also take my diary with me and check in with myself regularly so I am more aware of what is happening for me internally. I have my support networks outside of Bootcamp set up so I can contact them for emotional support as well. I hope to have check ins with my designated support person at Bootcamp and I am going to write a personalised well-being plan for myself to ensure that I take care of myself through the intensive learning.
